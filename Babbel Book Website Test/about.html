Our story

Built by parents,
for parents.

Babbel Books started as a late-night idea between two exhausted, book-loving parents — and a daughter who wouldn't stop telling stories.

Where it all began

Her name is Josephine Rose, and she changed everything.

From the very beginning, Josie — as we call her — was a babbler. Before she could form full sentences, she was stringing together sounds, gestures, and expressions that somehow told a complete story. We'd sit on the floor with her, completely transfixed, trying to piece together whatever epic adventure was unfolding in her little mind.

We're her parents — a couple of busy millennials trying to figure out this whole parenting thing one bedtime at a time. And like a lot of parents our age, we spend a lot of energy thinking about screens. Our phones are never far away. Tablets are everywhere. And the pressure to hand one over — just for five minutes of quiet — is very, very real.

"We didn't want technology to steal her attention span. We wanted it to light up her imagination."

The choice we made

We doubled down on bedtime reading.

We made a decision early on: books before bed, every night, no exceptions. Not because we had it all figured out, but because we'd read enough about early childhood development to know that those quiet moments — a warm lamp, a soft voice, a story — do something that no app can replicate. They build connection. They build imagination. They build the kind of focus that will matter for the rest of her life.

But one night, after reading the same board book for what felt like the forty-seventh time that week, we looked at each other and had an idea. What if Josie's story — her actual story, the one she'd been babbling about all afternoon — could become the book?

The idea

That's how Babbel Books was born.

We wanted to build something that used technology not as a distraction, but as a tool — a way to take the magical, chaotic, completely unrepeatable stories that children tell and turn them into something real. Something they could hold. Something you could read together, over and over again.

Every Babbel Book starts with a voice recording — your child, in their own words, telling their own story. We handle the rest: the writing, the illustrations, the printing, the delivery. What arrives at your door is a hardcover book that is genuinely, irreplaceably theirs.

The bigger picture

We're raising a generation that needs to know how to be offline.

We say this with love, because we include ourselves in it: our generation grew up watching the internet take over, and we handed smartphones to our kids before we fully understood what that meant. We're not anti-technology — clearly. But we do believe that for young children especially, the moments that matter most happen away from screens.

Sitting with your child and reading a book they made is one of those moments. It's not productive in any measurable way. It doesn't teach them to code or prepare them for some future career. It just reminds them — and you — that their voice matters. That their stories are worth telling. That being present together is the whole point.

If Babbel Books gets even a few more families to put the tablets down and crack open a book at bedtime, then Josie's babbling was worth every word.

"Your child's voice is the most creative thing in your house. We just help you keep it forever."
